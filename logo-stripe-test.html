Clean vs Striped — Analysis

  • Clean: Professional, safe, works everywhere. Risk: might be forgettable ("just blue text"). Best if you want pure restraint.
  • Striped: Distinctive IBM corporate modernism. More memorable, reinforces "governance layers" metaphor. Risk: busier, might feel dated in 10 years.
  • Legibility: Both are readable at small sizes. Stripes add texture but don't hurt readability at navbar size (28px).
  • Brand recall: Striped version is more likely to be remembered ("that IBM-style logo"). Clean blends in more.
  • For HN launch: Striped stands out more in a crowded "Show HN" thread. Clean is safer but less attention-grabbing.
  • For enterprise sales: Both work. Striped feels more "established" (IBM legacy). Clean feels more "modern SaaS".
