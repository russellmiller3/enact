ENΛCT — Corporate Modernist Edition (1960–1985)

Design Principle: Paul Rand, IBM striped logo, Wang wordmark, Swiss International Style, Bauhaus geometry. "Will still look authoritative in 40 years." Primary colors, geometric shapes, Helvetica, zero gradients. The Λ (missing crossbar) becomes the visual system — triangles, voids, structural absence.

ENΛCT
IBM Stripes
IBM Plex Sans on IBM blue with horizontal stripe pattern overlay. The stripes reference the classic 8-bar IBM logo (designed by Paul Rand, 1972). Maximum authority — "this company has been around since mainframes."
Reference: IBM striped wordmark, Paul Rand. Blue = trust, reliability, enterprise infrastructure. Stripes = speed, precision, data transmission.
ENΛCT
Helvetica Knockout
Pure Helvetica Bold, white on black, extreme letter-spacing. Swiss International Style at its most austere. Zero decoration. The missing A crossbar is the only graphic element needed.
Reference: Swiss International Typography, 1960s. Massimo Vignelli, NYC Subway signage. Black = authority. White knockout = clarity. Spacing = confidence.
ENΛCT
Triangle Mark
Red triangle (extracted from the Λ shape) as standalone mark. Wordmark below in Helvetica Black. The logo system separates: triangle = icon/favicon, wordmark = full identity.
Reference: Tandem Computers (1974), Bass Saul logos (AT&T, United Airlines). Red = action/urgency. Triangle = stability, hierarchy, the missing crossbar abstracted.
ENΛCT
Swiss Grid
Helvetica Black in thick black border with red accent bar. Architectural, modular, grid-based. Feels like it belongs in a 1970s technical manual or systems architecture diagram.
Reference: Swiss grid systems, technical documentation design (IBM, DEC, Wang). Red bar = status indicator, the "gate" in action firewall.
ENΛCT
Bauhaus Blocks
Black Roboto on yellow field with primary shape elements (red circle, blue square). Geometric, constructivist, educational. The shapes = modular building blocks.
Reference: Bauhaus school (1920s–30s), Herbert Bayer typography, László Moholy-Nagy. Yellow = optimism, visibility. Shapes = system components (policies, workflows, receipts).
ENΛCT
Corporate Wordmark
Helvetica Black in IBM blue with solid underline bar. Clean, professional, unmistakably enterprise. The underline = foundation, stability. Most conservative option.
Reference: Wang Laboratories, AT&T (pre-death-star), Digital Equipment Corporation. Blue bar = the infrastructure layer. Timeless, boring, reliable AF.

Recommendation: IBM Stripes (Option 1) or Swiss Grid (Option 4)

IBM Stripes if you want maximum "we are serious infrastructure" energy — the striped blue is instantly recognizable corporate modernism. Most distinctive, best brand recall.

Swiss Grid if you want architectural precision — the bordered box with red accent bar literally looks like a system diagram or architectural blueprint. Perfect for "deterministic policy engine" pitch.

Avoid: Bauhaus Blocks feels too playful for regulated industry buyers. Triangle Mark is strong but requires explaining the mark/wordmark relationship.
