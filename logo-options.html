ENΛCT Logo Options — Infrastructure-Grade Aesthetic

**ICP filter:** Platform engineers burned by agent disasters + CISOs approving governance budgets

**Positioning:** "Action firewall for AI agents" — Zapier + Okta + Splunk, not experimental startup

Horror stories: Replit deleted production DB, Kiro deleted EC2 systems. Logo must communicate "prevents disasters" not "cool AI experiment"

ENΛCT
Clean Infrastructure
Work Sans with generous spacing. White on dark = clean, serious, no decoration. Similar vibe to Cloudflare, Vercel, Railway — modern infra tools engineers trust.
For ICPs: Platform engineer sees "this is infrastructure." CISO sees "enterprise-grade tool." Hover expands spacing = feels responsive but restrained. Safe choice, won't alienate anyone.
ENΛCT
Policy Gate (Product Colors)
DM Sans with gradient: green → amber → red. Mirrors Enact's decision gates (PASS → REVIEW → BLOCK). Ties logo directly to product concept — the action firewall metaphor is visual.
For ICPs: Engineer immediately understands "this is about control/gates." Gradient is restrained (not neon). CISO sees "governance" not "flashy startup." Most distinctive option — memorable without being unprofessional.
ENΛCT
Code Confidence
JetBrains Mono (the IDE font) in cyan. Instantly signals "this is for engineers who write code." Monospace = precision, determinism, no surprises — matches "policy engine is pure Python" pitch.
For ICPs: Platform engineer feels at home (looks like their terminal/IDE). Cyan is technical but not aggressive. CISO risk: might feel too "dev tool" not "enterprise governance." Use if positioning as SDK-first, engineer-sold motion.
ENΛCT
Enterprise Gray
Manrope (geometric sans) with extreme letter-spacing and subtle blue underline. Maximum professionalism — feels like a tool that's been around for 10 years (in a good way).
For ICPs: CISO sees exactly what they expect from a $999/mo compliance tool. Engineer might find it boring, but won't question if it's "production-ready." Safest for enterprise sales, least distinctive for viral HN launch.
ENΛCT
Blueprint Technical
Archivo with layered shadow creating depth. Gray + blue = architectural plans, engineering precision. Technical without being "code" — appeals to platform engineers and DevOps leads.
For ICPs: Feels like infrastructure planning/design tools. "Blueprint" metaphor = deliberate, tested, reliable (matches "deterministic policy engine" pitch). Middle ground: professional but not boring, technical but not exclusionary.

My Recommendation: Policy Gate (Option 2)

Why: The gradient is the product — green (PASS), amber (HITL), red (BLOCK). It's distinctive without being unprofessional, immediately communicates "governance/control," and works for both ICPs: engineers see the decision logic, CISOs see traffic-light safety signaling.

Runner-up: Clean Infrastructure (Option 1) if you prioritize "safe/boring enterprise" over "memorable HN launch."

Avoid: Enterprise Gray feels generic (AgentBouncr territory). Code Monospace risks alienating CISO buyers.

Size Test: Policy Gate (Recommended)
